6. Explain the following concepts and methods and why they are needed (You must use YOUR OWN WORDS to answer the following terms. Copying texts from the lecture note/the Internet is strictly prohibited)¶

  • a. Text (pre)processing
  • b. Tokenization
  • c. Stopwords
  • d. Lowercase
  • e. Short words
  • f. Stemming
A: Text preprocessing is the technique of cleaning up and removing non alphanumeric characters from our text and turning the text into what we would call plain english¶
B: Tokenization is the process of splitting up each word in our text document into individual words, called tokens, so that we can analyze them.¶
C: Stopwords are words that appear frequently in language but do not really provide any meaningful information in terms of producing actionable information. Removing stopwords is necessary so that we can further refine our text document to just have words that we belive are important to our analysis.¶
D: Lowercasing words is the process of converting all of the words in our document to lowercase. This is an important step in NLP because python views uppercase and lowercase words, strings, and variables as different things, and we dont want to have cases of words appearing more than once because we didnt normalize our text by lowercasing words.¶
E: Short words are like the name implies, words that are short. Short words generally do not provide us much meaning, because they are words like and, but, or, if, so, etc. Since these words dont provide us any insights into what our text document is saying, we want to throw them out in order to better organize and clean our data for analysis.¶
F: Stemming is the process of removing the prefixes and suffixes of words to only get the root word for analysis. This is important again for many of the same reasons that lowercasing is. Python will interpret words like "map" and "mapping" and "mapped" to all be 3 different words, and this can cause an overrepresentation of a certain word or words in our analysis, making our results useless.¶
